There is something about a train that no flight can replicate. The slow reveal of a landscape through a window. The ritual of a station platform. The way a journey by rail makes you feel the actual distance between two places — and enjoy every kilometre of it.

2026 is shaping up to be a landmark year for European rail travel. New routes are opening, Interrail passes are more flexible than ever, and a growing wave of travellers are choosing the train not as compromise, but as the point. If you have been thinking about crossing Europe by rail, this is your year.

We have pulled together the best resources we could find — practical Interrail guides, scenic route round-ups, and expert advice from the people who have spent decades championing train travel in Europe.

## New routes launching in 2026

Every year brings new connections, but 2026 has a particularly strong lineup. Several routes that have been in planning for years are finally coming to life.

**[13 Of The Most Exciting New Train Routes Launching In 2026](https://www.timeout.com/travel/new-train-routes-launching-in-2026)** — *timeout.com*
A comprehensive overview of the brand-new rail connections opening this year, from high-speed links between major cities to scenic routes through regions that were previously hard to reach by train. Timeout have done the research so you do not have to.

## Interrail itineraries and pass advice

Planning an Interrail trip can feel overwhelming. How many travel days do you actually need? Which pass gives the best value? These guides cut through the noise.

**[Best Interrail Routes in Europe: Ultimate Three-Week Itinerary](https://www.timeout.com/travel/best-interrail-routes)** — *timeout.com*
If you have three weeks and a sense of adventure, this is the itinerary to start with. It covers the classic Western European circuit with some clever detours that most first-timers miss.

**[Experience Of A Lifetime — 7 Best Interrail Routes Through Europe](https://www.wanderlustchloe.com/interrail-routes/)** — *wanderlustchloe.com*
Chloe's routes lean towards the experiential — less about ticking off capital cities and more about finding the moments between them. Particularly strong on Southern and Central Europe.

**[InterRail Pass Guide 2026](https://www.seat61.com/InterRail-pass-guide.htm)** — *seat61.com*
The definitive breakdown of pass types, pricing, and how to actually use them. Seat61 is the gold standard for European rail information, and this guide is updated annually with the latest changes.

**[The Ultimate Interrail Itinerary for Eastern Europe](https://www.anadventurousworld.com/interrail-eastern-europe/)** — *anadventurousworld.com*
Eastern Europe remains one of the most underrated Interrail regions. This itinerary covers Romania, Bulgaria, Serbia, and beyond — places where your money goes further and the crowds have not arrived yet.

## The most scenic rides

Sometimes the journey genuinely is the destination. These routes are chosen for what you see from the window.

**[The Most Beautiful Train Rides in Europe (2026)](https://www.findingalexx.com/beautiful-train-rides-europe/)** — *findingalexx.com*
A visual feast of Alpine passes, Norwegian fjord railways, and Mediterranean coastal lines. Each route includes practical details on booking and timing for the best views.

**[The Top 6 Train Routes to Try in 2026](https://www.interrail.eu/en/inspiration/top-train-routes-2026)** — *interrail.eu*
Interrail's own pick of the routes they think define this year. These are chosen not just for scenery but for the overall experience — the stops along the way, the local food, the accommodation options.

**[The Top 6 Train Routes to Try in 2026](https://www.eurail.com/en/get-inspired/top-train-routes-2026)** — *eurail.com*
Eurail's companion list, aimed at non-European travellers. Similar routes but with additional context on how to combine them with Eurail passes and which connections work best for visitors coming from outside Europe.

## The essential reference

If there is one website every European rail traveller should bookmark, it is this one.

**[The Man in Seat 61](https://www.seat61.com/)** — *seat61.com*
Mark Smith has spent over two decades documenting how to travel by train across Europe and beyond. His site is the single most comprehensive, practical, and lovingly maintained resource for train travel anywhere on the internet. Whether you need to know which platform to find the sleeper train to Vienna or how to book a scenic route through the Balkans, start here.
