The best travel memories almost always involve a table. A plate of something you did not expect. A flavour that rewired your understanding of what a familiar ingredient could be. Travel and food are inseparable — and in 2026, more destinations than ever are putting their culinary identity front and centre.

Whether you plan trips around Michelin stars or prefer to find the best street food stall in a city you have never visited, this curated guide brings together the resources worth reading before you book.

## The destination guides

Where should you actually go if food is the reason you travel? These guides approach the question from different angles — Michelin's authority, Timeout's global reach, and specialist blogs that go deeper into specific regions.

**[The Best Places to Travel in 2026 for Food Lovers: 16 Must-Visit Destinations](https://guide.michelin.com/en/article/travel/best-places-to-travel-in-2026-for-food-lovers)** — *guide.michelin.com*
When Michelin publishes a destination list, it carries weight. This is not just about starred restaurants — it covers cities where the entire food culture is worth the trip, from Tokyo's kissaten coffee shops to Lima's cevicherias. The 16 picks span every continent and every budget.

**[Where to Go in North America for Food Lovers in 2026](https://guide.michelin.com/en/article/travel/where-to-go-in-north-america-for-food-lovers-in-2026)** — *guide.michelin.com*
A focused companion piece for North America. If you are planning a US or Mexico trip and want to build it around eating well, this narrows the field to the cities and regions that are genuinely leading right now.

**[The World's Best Food Destinations For 2026 Travel](https://www.timeout.com/travel/best-food-destinations)** — *timeout.com*
Timeout's take is more adventurous and street-level than Michelin's. Expect unexpected picks — cities where the food scene is exploding but has not yet been canonised by the guidebooks. A great counterweight to the established lists.

**[Best Travel Destinations For Food Lovers](https://www.wanderlustchloe.com/best-travel-destinations-food/)** — *wanderlustchloe.com*
Chloe Gunning's picks lean personal and photogenic. If you want food destinations that also photograph beautifully — markets with character, restaurants with views, regions with visual drama — this is where to start.

## Slow food, farm stays, and the deeper experience

Not every food trip needs to be a restaurant crawl. Some of the most memorable culinary travel happens when you slow right down — staying on a working farm, eating what is grown around you, learning to cook from the people who know the land.

**[Best Organic Agriturismo Farm Stays in 2026](https://farmstayplanet.com/best-organic-agriturismo-farm-stays/)** — *farmstayplanet.com*
Italy's agriturismo tradition is one of the great inventions of modern travel. This guide covers the best organic farm stays where you eat what is grown on site — handmade pasta with flour from the estate, olive oil pressed that morning, wine from the vineyard you can see from your bedroom window.

## The essential reading

Before you go anywhere, read this.

**[The Food Traveler's Handbook by Jodi Ettenberg](https://www.legalnomads.com/food-travel/)** — *legalnomads.com*
Jodi Ettenberg literally wrote the book on food travel. Her handbook covers the practical side — how to find great food in unfamiliar places, how to navigate dietary restrictions abroad, how to eat adventurously without getting ill. It is the kind of resource that changes how you approach every trip, not just the next one.
